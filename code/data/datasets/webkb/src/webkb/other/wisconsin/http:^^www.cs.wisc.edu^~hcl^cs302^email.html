E-mail Information

Electronic mail is a convenient way for people to communicate, and will sometimes be used to disseminate information to class members between class meetings (the home page will be used first, though). Students are also encouraged to communicate via e-mail using either the e-mail list, or by sending e-mail directly to me. When sending e-mail, make certain that you have a brief, informative subject line, so that others can easily determine the content of your message.

Class E-mail List

Our class has a "mailing list" associated with it that we will use this semester. The e-mail address is cs302-13list. E-mail sent to this e-mail list will be received by me and all class members registered for this section. You may also send e-mail to this list, and you are encouraged to do so for the following purposes:

  • Forming study groups, or
  • discussion of course material (I may or may not participate in such discussion, but such discussions are often useful to the participants as well as onlookers).

Sending E-mail to me ( hcl@cs.wisc.edu)

You are welcome to send e-mail to me, and I will do my best to answer your questions or other concerns promptly. However, to avoid being swamped by e-mail, I will follow this e-mail reply policy:
  • Office visits take priority over e-mail. Thus if I have e-mail pending and also students waiting at my office, I will attend to the students first and then e-mail.
  • I will give priority to e-mail of general concern. Thus e-mail pertaining to important/relevant class material or problems with my assignments will be handled first. Questions that are narrow in scope, such as debugging problems that pertain to an individual student, will be handled when possible (consultants in the lab should be your first line of attack).
  • I can't guarantee that I will reply to all e-mail. Though, I will try my best to do so.
