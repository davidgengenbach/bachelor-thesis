Nikolaos Ioannis Moshovos
Poet

Nikolaos Moshovos was born in 1964 at Thessaloniki, Greece. He spent most of his childhood in Athens and in Serres. In 1974 he returns to Thessaloniki where he starts writting poems and prose.

In 1992, his early poems are published. His collection of poems titled "Agnantevontas ta Oneira, Mia Poiitiki Diadromi 1974-1991" draws the attention of several literature related publications and of newspapers (NOYMAS, ANTI, ELEYTHERO PNEYMA, GIATI, MAKEDONIA, ELEYSIS etc.). His second collection of poems, "To petagma toy Ianou " was published in November 1995.

Many artists have commented on his work. Among them are: M. Kougioumtzis, Akis Gerontakis, Michalis Peristeraki, Michalis Katsaros, Lampros Malamas, and Xrisoula Varveri-Varra.

He is a member of 15 international and national literature organizations from Greece, France, India, Israel, Italy, and U.S.A.

He has has received several awards: DEEL - Greece, Premio San Benedetto Patrono D'Europa, Academia Fiorino - Italy, 2nd literature award of the Lutece International Academy - France and others. His poem Myrto won the second place award in the International Poetry Contest of the Valentiana Academy, Italy. Finaly, he is the coordinator of the World Poetry Congress that is going to take place in 2005 at Thessaloniki, Greece.
