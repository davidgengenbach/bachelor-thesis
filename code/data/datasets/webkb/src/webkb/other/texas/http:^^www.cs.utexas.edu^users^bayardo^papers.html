Journal Articles

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr. and Daniel P. Miranker. An optimal backtrack algorithm for tree-structured constraint satisfaction problems. Artificial Intelligence 71(1), 159-181, 1994.

    Presents a top-down algorithm for solving tree-structured constraint satisfaction problems in linear time and evaluates its average-case performance empirically.
    (Not available on-line)

Conference Papers

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr. and Daniel P. Miranker. Processing Queries for First-Few Answers. In Proc. of the Fifth Int'l Conf. on Information and Knowledge Management , 45-52, Rockville, Maryland, 1996.

    Describes a probabalistic method for predicting costs of first-answer query plans under a modified pipelined-join execution model.
    cikm96.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 92k)

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr. and Robert Schrag. Using CSP look-back techniques to solve exceptionally hard SAT instances. In Proc. of the Second Int'l Conf. on Principles and Practice of Constraint Programming (Lecture Notes in Computer Science 1118) , 46-60, Springer, 1996.

    CSP look-back techniques (CBJ, learning), not widely used in existing SAT algorithms, turn out to be indispensable for solving instances which are "exceptionally hard". A new procedure we define generates such instances consistently.
    cp96.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 90k)
    Procedures used in this paper are available in the toolkit linked below. Click here for a brief description of its contents.
    ehi_tool.tar.Z (v1.01) (Tar'ed and compressed SPARC executables, 298k)

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr. and Daniel P. Miranker. A complexity analysis of space-bounded learning algorithms for the constraint satisfaction problem. In Proc. of the 13th National Conf. on Artificial Intelligence, 298-304, 1996.

    Analyzes the effects of space-bounded learning (a.k.a. constraint-recording) schemes on the runtime complexity of backtrack search.
    aaai96.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 97k)

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr. and Daniel P. Miranker. On the space-time trade-off in solving constraint satisfaction problems. In Proc. of the 14th Int'l Joint Conf. on Artificial Intelligence, 558-562, 1995.

    From the perspective of achieving tractable constraint satisfaction by restricting constraint graph structure, extensive use of space may be of limited benefit.
    ijcai95.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 47k)

  • J. C. Browne, A. Emerson, M. G. Gouda, D. P. Miranker, A. Mok, R. J. Bayardo Jr., S. Chodrow, D. Gadbois, F. Haddix, T. W. Hetherington, L. Obermeyer, D.-C. Tsou, C.-K. Wang, and R. Wang. A new approach to modularity in rule-based programming. In Proc. of the Sixth Int'l Conf. on Tools with Artificial Intelligence, 18-25, IEEE Press, 1994.

    Describes a purely declarative method for introducing modularity into forward-chaining rule-based languages, and its embodiment in the syntax of the Venus rule language.
    ictai94.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 372k)

Refereed Workshop Papers

  • D. P. Miranker, Roberto J. Bayardo Jr., and V. Samoladas. Query Evaluation as Constraint Search; An Overview of Early Results. In Proc. of the Workshop on Constraints and Databases at the Second Int'l Conf. on Principles and Practice of Constraint Programming (Lecture Notes in Computer Science, volume to be determined), Springer, 1997.

    Overview of various results on applying constraint processing techniques to query processing.
    cp96db_lncs.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 62k)

Technical Reports

  • R. J. Bayardo Jr., B. Bohrer, R. Brice, A. Cichocki, J. Fowler, S. Helal, V. Kashyap, T. Ksiezyk, G. Martin, M. Nodine, M. Rashid, M. Rusinkiewicz, R. Shea, C. Unnikrishnan, A. Unruh, and D. Woelk. Infosleuth: Semantic Integration of Information in Open and Dynamic Environments. Technical Report MCC-INSL-088-96, Microelectronics and Computer Technology Corporation, 1996.

    Describes agent-based architecture for integrating heterogeneous information bases in an ever-changing network of information sources.
    (PostScript available through the InfoSleuth Project .)

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr.. Dealing with Duplicate Tuples in Multi-Join Query Processing. Technical Report TR-96-11, University of Texas at Austin, Dept. of Computer Sciences, May 1996.

    Presents and evaluates several schemes for handling duplicate tuple elimination during optimization and execution of large select-project-join queries.
    TR-96-11.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 67k)

  • Roberto J. Bayardo Jr.. Enhancing Query Plans for Many-Way Joins. Technical Report TR-95-09, University of Texas at Austin, Dept. of Computer Sciences, March 1995.

    Proposes a method for annotating query plans with additional operators in order to efficiently produce the query result in the form of a directionally reduced acyclic database.
    TR-95-09.ps.Z (Compressed PostScript, 72k)
