Course Topics

The goal of this course is to present a number of important ideas in distributed computing and provide rigorous methods for reasoning about distributed programs. The topics are:
  • Distributed detection algorithms
  • Distributed resource allocation
  • Logical clocks
  • Global snapshot
  • Logics for reasoning about distributed algorithms
  • Safety and progress
  • Program composition
  • Distributed program construction

Discussion Sessions

The discussion sessions will allow us to discuss topics presented in class in detail and on occasion to cover background material. Weekly handouts containing exercises will be distributed (in class and on the Web) and selected solutions will be presented during the discussion sessions. You only need attend one of the weekly sessions; both cover the same material.

Study Groups

We recommend that you organize yourselves into study groups of five or six students who meet once a week to discuss the course. You should review the lectures, do the readings, and attempt the homework assignments before meeting as a group.
