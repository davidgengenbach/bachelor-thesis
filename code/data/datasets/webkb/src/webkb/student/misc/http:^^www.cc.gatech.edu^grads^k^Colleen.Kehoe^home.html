I'm currently a Ph.D. student in the Graphics, Visualization, and Usability (GVU) Center in the College of Computing at Georgia Tech. I graduated in 1994 with a B.S. in Computer Science from Stevens Institute of Technology in Hoboken, NJ. While I was at Stevens, I was in the Co-Op program, so I've had the chance to work at a variety of companies:
  • IBM (Poughkeepsie, NY) - TSO/E Development & Testing
  • AT&T Bell Laboratories (Murray Hill, NJ) - Electroacoustics Research Tools
  • Dialogic Corp. (Piscataway, NJ) - PBX Tone Collection System
  • AT&T Bell Laboratories (Murray Hill, NJ) - Application Level Security for Distributed Systems
  • AT&T Bell Laboratories (Murray Hill, NJ) - Handwriting Recognition Project

Current Projects

GVU's WWW User Surveys
In our spare time, Jim Pitkow and I have been working on these surveys. Results from the Fourth Survey are now available. My most recent survey-related effort is working on a prototype in Java. Beta coming soon.
Multimedia Courseware for Computer Science Education
For this project, we're designing a large set of web pages (and tools for creating & organizing them) that will be a resource for an introductory graphics course. The pages aren't quite ready for public consumption, but should be soon.
Educational Applications of Visualizations
I'm continuing a project I started this summer with John Stasko to evaluate the use of animation in teaching algorithms. Here's an online version of a section of Corman, Leiserson, and Rivest's explanation of binomial heaps with a few animations thrown in. Sorry, CoC folks only.
Local Java Efforts
I am (attempting to) maintain a page that points to some of the Java applets done by students here at Tech.
Cyberguide
My newest project is working on Cyberguide with Gregory Abowd and the FCE bunch. I figured I'd better do something with that Newton I bought. (Ha ha.)
